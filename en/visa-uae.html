Introduction

The UAE — comprising Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Sharjah, and 4 other emirates — is one of the world's top destinations for tourism, business, and relocation. Dubai is a global aviation hub (DXB), and the UAE has an expansive visa-free and visa-on-arrival policy. Most Western nationalities are visa-free for 30 days. The UAE introduced a revamped visa system in 2022 making it easier than ever to visit and relocate.

Tourist & Short Stay

  • Visa on Arrival (Free, 30 days): Citizens of 50+ countries including USA, UK, EU (Schengen), Canada, Australia, Japan, South Korea, Singapore, Malaysia, and many others receive a free 30-day visa on arrival, extendable once for another 30 days (AED 600 fee). 180 days per year total.
  • eVisa 30 days — AED 250: For nationalities not covered by VoA. Apply via ICA portal (icp.gov.ae), airline websites (Emirates, Etihad), or travel agents. Single or multiple entry. Processing 3–5 business days.
  • eVisa 60 days — AED 350: Double the stay; same application process. Multiple-entry options available.
  • 96-hour Visa (Free): For transit passengers connecting through DXB/AUH — apply via Emirates/Etihad. Allows hotel stay in Dubai/Abu Dhabi.
  • 5-year Multiple-Entry Tourist Visa: Available for eligible nationalities — AED 650. Allows multiple visits, 90 days per entry, 180 days/year max.

Business Visa

Business visits (meetings, exhibitions) are covered by tourist visa or VoA for most nationalities. For long-term business, the UAE offers:

  • Free Zone company formation (0% corporate tax, 100% foreign ownership) in DIFC, ADGM, JAFZA, and 40+ others.
  • Mainland company (Limited Liability Company or branch) with a local service agent.
  • Dubai CommerCity, Abu Dhabi Global Market — sector-specific business hubs.

Work & Long-Stay Visas

  • Employment Visa (2-year, renewable): Employer-sponsored; employer files with Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation (MOHRE). Employee undergoes Emirates ID biometrics and medical check. Salary minimum varies by emirate and job category.
  • Golden Visa (10 years): For investors (AED 2 million+ in property or business), entrepreneurs, specialized talents (doctors, engineers, scientists, artists), and outstanding students. Apply via icp.gov.ae. No sponsor required; 6-month multiple-entry interim visa while processing.
  • Green Visa (5 years, self-sponsored): For skilled employees (min. salary AED 15,000/month), freelancers, and investors in companies. No employer sponsor needed.
  • Freelancer/Remote Work Visa (1 year): Apply via Dubai Virtual Work Programme (virtualworkers.dubai.gov.ae). AED 611 + medical/Emirates ID fees. For remote workers employed abroad earning USD 3,500+/month.

Student Visa

Student residence visa required for full-time university enrollment. Sponsored by the university; issued for 1 year, renewable. Allows part-time work (20 hours/week) with employer NOC. Medical examination and Emirates ID mandatory.

Airport Transit

Most nationalities may transit airside at DXB or AUH without a visa. Landside transit (leaving airport) requires either VoA eligibility, eVisa, or the free 96-hour transit visa (via airline). Dubai and Abu Dhabi are global layover destinations with dedicated transit hotels and tours.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport (6 months validity)
  • eVisa approval or VoA eligibility
  • Return or onward flight ticket
  • Hotel booking or invitation letter from UAE resident
  • Proof of sufficient funds
  • Travel insurance (recommended)
  • For work visa: medical certificate, police clearance, qualifications, employment contract
  • For Golden Visa: proof of investment/talent/academic achievement

How to Apply

  1. Check if you qualify for visa-free/VoA at icp.gov.ae or ask your airline.
  2. For eVisa: apply via icp.gov.ae (Federal Authority for Identity, Citizenship, Customs and Port Security) or through Emirates/Etihad/Flydubai websites.
  3. Upload passport scan, photo, and pay online. Receive eVisa by email in 3–5 business days.
  4. For Golden Visa or Green Visa: apply at icp.gov.ae; gather investment documents, medical clearance, and pay fees.
  5. For work visa: your employer initiates the process at MOHRE (mohre.gov.ae); you receive an entry permit, travel to UAE, complete medical exam and Emirates ID enrollment.

Who Gets Free Visa on Arrival?

50+ nationalities receive free 30-day VoA including: USA, UK, all EU/Schengen countries, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, South Korea, Singapore, Malaysia, and many others. GCC nationals (Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman, Qatar) enter freely on national ID. All others use the eVisa system (AED 250–350). Holders of valid USA/UK/Schengen visas may also qualify for a VoA — check icp.gov.ae for the full list.

Always verify current visa rules on official government sites before travel.

Introduction

The Maldives is one of the world's premier luxury tourism destinations, known for its overwater bungalows, coral reefs, and crystal-clear lagoons. The Maldives operates a uniquely open visa policy — virtually all nationalities receive a free tourist visa on arrival for 30 days, making it one of the easiest countries to visit.

Tourist & Short Stay

  • Visa on Arrival (Free) — 30 days: All nationalities with a valid passport, return ticket, confirmed accommodation (resort or guesthouse booking), and proof of sufficient funds (USD 100/day minimum) receive a free 30-day visa on arrival at Velana International Airport. No prior application required.
  • Extension — 60 additional days: Apply at Maldives Immigration online portal (immigration.gov.mv) for an extension up to 90 days total. Fee: USD 30 (first 30-day extension) + USD 30 (second extension). Maximum stay 90 days per visit.
  • Online Pre-Registration (IMUGA): While not mandatory, the Maldives recommends using the IMUGA passenger portal (imuga.immigration.gov.mv) for health declaration and faster processing.

Business Visa

Business visits (meetings, investment discussions) are conducted under the tourist visa on arrival. For longer-term business operations, companies must register with the Maldives Economic Development Ministry. A business visa (90-day, extendable) can be applied for from the Maldives Immigration department for investors and business professionals.

Work & Long-Stay Visas

  • Work Visa / Expatriate Permit: Foreign workers must be sponsored by a Maldivian employer registered with the Ministry of Economic Development. The employer applies for an employment permit; the employee then gets a work visa from Immigration. Common in hospitality, diving, construction sectors.
  • Investor Visa: For significant investors in Maldives tourism projects or Free Trade Zone projects. Minimum investment threshold varies by project type.

Student Visa

Student visa available for formal education at Maldives universities or colleges. Apply with institution acceptance, proof of funds, and accommodation details. Limited education infrastructure — most higher education seekers study abroad.

Airport Transit

Transit passengers at Velana International who do not leave the airport do not need a visa. Those wishing to visit Malé or a resort during transit require the standard VoA (free).

Required Documents

  • Valid passport (at least 1 month validity at entry)
  • Return or onward ticket
  • Confirmed resort or guesthouse booking
  • Proof of sufficient funds (USD 100/day minimum; credit card acceptable)
  • Yellow fever vaccination certificate (if arriving from endemic country)
  • IMUGA health declaration (recommended; may be mandatory periodically)

How to Apply

  1. Book your resort or guesthouse — confirmed accommodation is mandatory at entry.
  2. (Optional) Complete IMUGA health declaration at imuga.immigration.gov.mv before travel.
  3. Arrive at Velana International Airport (MLE). Proceed to immigration.
  4. Present passport, return ticket, and accommodation confirmation.
  5. Receive free 30-day tourist visa stamp — no advance application needed.
  6. For extension: apply at immigration.gov.mv before your initial 30 days expire.

Who Gets a Free Visa on Arrival?

All nationalities receive a free 30-day tourist visa on arrival — there are no exclusions based on nationality (provided all requirements are met: valid passport, return ticket, accommodation, and funds). This makes the Maldives one of the world's most accessible destinations in terms of visa policy. Exceptions: citizens of countries under specific international sanctions may face additional checks.

Always verify current visa rules on official government sites before travel.

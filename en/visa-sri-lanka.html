Introduction

Sri Lanka is an island nation off the southern tip of India, known for its beaches, ancient ruins, tea plantations, and wildlife. Almost all visitors require an Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) before arrival, obtained online at eta.gov.lk. Sri Lanka has periodically offered free ETAs to boost tourism.

Tourist & Short Stay

  • ETA (Tourist) — USD 20–35: Required for almost all nationalities. Apply at eta.gov.lk. Approval typically within minutes–24 hours. Allows 30-day initial stay, extendable up to 6 months total at a Department of Immigration office.
  • Free ETA: Sri Lanka has periodically waived ETA fees for citizens of certain countries (India, China, Russia, Indonesia, Thailand, and others at various times). Check eta.gov.lk for current free-ETA nationalities.
  • Visa on Arrival: Available for select nationalities at CMB airport — the same ETA fee applies; it is easier to apply online in advance.
  • Maldives, Singapore nationals: Traditionally enjoy free/reduced-fee ETA or visa-free access under bilateral agreements.

Business Visa

ETA (Business) — USD 35. Allows 30-day stay for business meetings, conferences, and trade activities. For long-term business investment, a Board of Investment (BOI) permit is required, which can lead to a Resident Guest visa for investors committing USD 250,000+.

Work & Long-Stay Visas

  • Resident Guest Visa: For investors and long-term residents. Requires significant investment or employment by a BOI-approved company.
  • Employment Visa: Employer must register with the Department of Immigration; visa issued for the duration of the employment contract. USD 50–100 fee.
  • Retirement (Resident Guest): Sri Lanka offers a Resident Guest visa for retirees with income of at least USD 1,500/month or a deposit of USD 18,000/year in a Sri Lankan bank.

Student Visa

Student visa required for formal enrollment in Sri Lanka institutions. Apply at Department of Immigration with acceptance letter, proof of funds, and medical clearance. Generally issued for the duration of study.

Airport Transit

Transit without visa is permitted for most nationalities remaining airside at CMB. Passengers clearing immigration need a valid ETA or visa.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport (6 months validity)
  • ETA approval reference number (online application)
  • Return or onward ticket
  • Proof of accommodation
  • Proof of sufficient funds (USD 50/day minimum recommended)
  • Travel and health insurance
  • Yellow fever vaccination certificate (if arriving from endemic country)

How to Apply

  1. Visit eta.gov.lk — the official Sri Lanka ETA portal.
  2. Select "Tourist" or "Business" ETA, complete personal and travel details.
  3. Upload a scan of your passport bio-data page.
  4. Pay the fee online (Visa/Mastercard accepted).
  5. Receive ETA approval by email — print or save the reference number.
  6. Present the ETA reference on arrival at CMB — it is linked to your passport.

Who Needs an ETA?

Almost all nationalities require an ETA to enter Sri Lanka. Some countries receive free ETAs (India, China, Russia, Indonesia, Thailand and others — the list changes seasonally). SAARC member state citizens (India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Nepal, Bhutan, Maldives, Afghanistan) may have reduced fees or free ETA. Always check eta.gov.lk for the current fee structure before travel.

Always verify current visa rules on official government sites before travel.

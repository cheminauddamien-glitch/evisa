Vietnam Visa Processing Time 2026: Timelines, Tracking & Tips

How long does a Vietnam visa take in 2026? Standard processing is 3 business days. This guide covers all visa types, peak-season delays, tracking methods, and tips to avoid common hold-ups.

Processing Times by Visa Type — Vietnam 2026

Processing Times — Vietnam
Standard eVisa (online)3 business days
Consular visa (embassy)5–10 business days
Peak season (Tet, summer)Up to 5 business days
Visa-free entry (13 nationalities)Instant — no application needed
Work permit (employer-applied)15–30 working days
Temporary Residence Card7–14 working days after work permit

Processing times are estimates and may vary. Always apply with adequate lead time to account for unexpected delays. Official tracking: evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn.

When to Apply for a Vietnam Visa

Apply as early as possible — most Vietnam visa applications can be submitted up to 3–6 months before travel. The minimum lead time is the standard processing period of 3 business days, but applying earlier gives you time to correct any document errors, request missing records, or re-apply if necessary.

Peak travel periods (summer holidays, national festivals, Christmas/New Year) can double processing times at consulates and application centres. If you are travelling during these periods, submit your application at least 6–8 weeks in advance.

How to Track Your Vietnam Visa Application

Most Vietnam visa applications can be tracked online using your application reference number. After submitting, you should receive:

  • An email confirmation with your reference number
  • A link to the online tracking portal at evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn
  • Email and/or SMS updates at key stages (receipt, decision, collection)

If no update is received within the expected processing period, contact the processing centre using your reference number. Do not book non-refundable flights or accommodation until your visa is confirmed.

Common Causes of Processing Delays

The most common reasons for Vietnam visa processing delays are: incomplete documentation, passport photos not meeting official specifications, insufficient financial proof, failure to provide certified translations, high application volumes during peak season, and discrepancies between the application form and supporting documents. Use the official checklist from evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn and double-check all details before submission.

Introduction

New Zealand is renowned for its landscapes, adventure tourism, and outdoor activities. Visitors from visa-waiver countries must obtain a New Zealand Electronic Travel Authority (NZeTA) before travel — there is no visa-free entry without it. A separate International Visitor Conservation and Tourism Levy (IVL) of NZD 35 is also charged at the same time.

Tourist & Short Stay

  • NZeTA (for visa-waiver countries) — NZD 23 (app) or NZD 17 (web) + NZD 35 IVL: Required for citizens of UK, USA, Canada, EU countries, Australia, Japan, South Korea, Singapore and ~60 others. Apply at nzeta.immigration.govt.nz or via the NZeTA app. Valid for multiple entries over 2 years; each stay up to 90 days (UK/Irish: up to 6 months).
  • Visitor Visa — NZD 211: For nationalities not on the visa-waiver list. Apply at immigration.govt.nz/visaview. Multiple-entry possible; stay up to 9 months (citizens of UK/certain Pacific).
  • Australian citizens: May enter visa-free, no NZeTA needed — use Australian passport on arrival.

Business Visa

Short business visits (meetings, trade events) are covered by NZeTA or Visitor visa. For investment, the Investor Visa (subclass requires NZD 3 million+ or NZD 15 million+) and Entrepreneur Work Visa support longer business engagement. An Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV) is required for paid employment by New Zealand companies.

Work & Long-Stay Visas

  • Accredited Employer Work Visa (AEWV): Employer must be NZBN-accredited; job offer required; minimum skill level ANZSCO 4+. Duration up to 3 years. Pathway to residence via Green List or Skilled Migrant Category.
  • Working Holiday Visa: Ages 18–30 (some 35); NZD 320; 12 months (up to 23 months for UK/Irish); work in any job; available from ~45 countries.
  • Skilled Migrant Category (SMC): Expression of Interest (EOI) points system; skilled employment in NZ required; leads to permanent residence.
  • Green List Occupations: Direct pathway to residence for specific shortage occupations (e.g., nurses, engineers) with job offer at or above median wage.

Student Visa

Fee NZD 375. Requires offer from a New Zealand Qualifications Framework (NZQF) registered provider, proof of funds (NZD 15,000/year), and medical/chest X-ray if from high-risk country. Partners may apply for an open work visa. Part-time work (20 hours/week) permitted during study.

Airport Transit

Transit passengers who remain in the international zone do not need a visa. If you need to clear immigration or change airports, a transit visa or standard visa is required.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport (minimum 3 months beyond departure)
  • NZeTA approval or visitor visa grant letter
  • Return or onward travel ticket
  • Proof of funds (NZD 1,000/month or NZD 400/month if accommodation pre-paid)
  • Accommodation details or host invitation
  • Travel and health insurance (recommended)
  • For work/student: job offer, employer accreditation, or enrolment letter

신청 방법

  1. Check if your country is on the visa-waiver list at immigration.govt.nz.
  2. If yes: apply for NZeTA and pay IVL at nzeta.immigration.govt.nz or via the NZeTA mobile app.
  3. If no: apply for a Visitor visa through immigration.govt.nz/visaview (create a RealMe account).
  4. Upload required documents and pay the fee online.
  5. Receive NZeTA/visa approval by email (linked to your passport).
  6. Present your passport on arrival — no stamp or label needed for NZeTA.

Who Needs an NZeTA vs. Visa?

Australian citizens: no NZeTA needed — enter visa-free. Visa-waiver countries (USA, UK, EU, Canada, Japan, Singapore etc.): must obtain NZeTA + IVL before travel. All other nationalities: apply for Visitor visa (NZD 211). Some Pacific Island citizens have extended rights under special bilateral agreements.

Always verify current visa rules on official government sites before travel.

🏥 Health & Entry Requirements for New Zealand

No mandatory vaccinations but certain nationalities require medical and chest X-ray. TB screening for stays 6+ months (except low-risk countries). Health insurance strongly recommended - NZ has reciprocal healthcare agreements with UK and Australia only. INZ-approved panel physicians required for medical exams. Police clearance for stays 24+ months.

🕑 Visa Extension & Overstay Rules in New Zealand

Visitor visa extendable at INZ, max 9 months in 18. Must apply before current visa expires. Overstay: section 289 deportation liability, 5-year entry ban. Voluntary departure reduces penalties. Must comply with visa conditions (no work on visitor visa).

❌ Visa Refusal & Appeals for New Zealand

Common: insufficient funds, health concerns, character issues, genuine intent doubts. Appeal to Immigration and Protection Tribunal (IPT) for residence visas. Reconsideration request for other visas. Immigration advisers must be licensed by IAA.

US B1/B2 Visa for Nigerian Citizens

Nigerian passport holders require a B1/B2 non-immigrant visitor visa to enter the United States for tourism, medical treatment, or business. The application fee is USD 185 (MRV fee), paid online before scheduling an interview at the US Embassy or Consulate.

How to Apply for a US B1/B2 Visa in 2026

  1. Complete DS-160 (Online Nonimmigrant Visa Application) at ceac.state.gov.
  2. Pay the USD 185 MRV fee via ustraveldocs.com.
  3. Schedule an interview at the nearest US Embassy or Consulate.
  4. Attend the interview with all required documents.
  5. If approved, your passport will be returned with the visa within 3–10 business days.

Current interview wait times for Nigerian citizens: approximately 3–8 weeks — apply well in advance.

Required Documents for the Visa Interview

  • Valid Nigerian passport (min. 6 months validity beyond intended stay)
  • DS-160 confirmation page
  • USD 185 MRV fee receipt
  • One passport-sized photo (5 cm × 5 cm, white background)
  • Proof of ties to home country (employment letter, property deeds, family evidence)
  • Bank statements / financial evidence
  • Travel itinerary and accommodation bookings

Tips to Strengthen Your US Visa Application

The consular officer will assess your non-immigrant intent — your intention to return home after your US visit. Strong evidence of ties to your home country (stable employment, family, property ownership) significantly improves approval chances. Provide honest, consistent answers and bring comprehensive financial documentation.

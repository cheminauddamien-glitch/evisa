Overview of Sweden Visa Requirements 2026

Sweden is a Schengen Area member. Visitors who require a visa must apply for a Schengen short-stay visa (Type C), which allows stays of up to 90 days within any 180-day period across all Schengen member states. Applications are processed through the Sweden embassy or consulate in your country of residence, or via an authorised VFS Global visa application centre.

The Sweden consulate assesses applications on the basis of genuine visitor intent, financial self-sufficiency, ties to your home country, and the completeness of your document file. Submitting a well-organised, complete application significantly reduces the risk of refusal or delays.

Documents Checklist for Sweden Visa 2026

  • Valid passport — at least 3 months validity beyond stay, issued within the last 10 years, minimum 2 blank pages
  • Completed and signed visa application form (downloadable from swedenabroad.se)
  • Two recent passport photos — 35×45mm, white background, neutral expression, taken within the last 6 months
  • Travel insurance — minimum EUR 30,000 coverage, valid for the full duration of stay, covering all Schengen countries
  • Proof of accommodation — hotel booking, rental agreement, or host invitation letter
  • Return flight booking or travel itinerary
  • Financial proof — bank statements for the last 3–6 months, payslips, or sponsor letter
  • Proof of employment, business registration, or student enrolment
  • Visa fee payment — EUR 80 (non-refundable)

Eligibility Criteria for Sweden Visa 2026

Applicants must demonstrate that their visit is temporary and that they intend to return to their home country before the visa expires. Key eligibility factors assessed by the Sweden consulate include: strong ties to the home country (employment, property, family), sufficient financial means, a clean immigration and travel history, valid travel insurance, and a clear purpose of visit.

Certain nationalities benefit from visa-free access to Sweden and the Schengen Area. Check the official swedenabroad.se website or IATA Travel Centre to confirm whether you need a visa before applying.

How to Apply for a Sweden Visa in 2026

Book an appointment at the Sweden embassy, consulate, or VFS Global visa application centre in your country. Bring all required documents (originals and photocopies), complete the application form, and pay the EUR 80 fee. Biometrics (fingerprints and photo) will be collected at the appointment for first-time applicants. The standard processing time is 15 working days (up to 45 days in complex cases). Apply between 6 months before travel and 4–6 weeks before travel.

Hungary for Mexican Citizens — Overview

Mexican passport holders can visit Hungary visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period. From 2025, an ETIAS travel authorisation (EUR 7, valid 3 years) is required before entry.

ETIAS — Key Details

ETIAS (European Travel Information and Authorisation System) is the EU's mandatory pre-travel check for visa-exempt travellers. It is not a visa — it is linked to your passport electronically.

  • Cost: EUR 7
  • Validity: 3 years or until passport expiry
  • Covers all Schengen Area countries including Hungary
  • Processing: usually within minutes; up to 4 days
  • Multiple entries permitted
  • Apply online at: travel-europe.europa.eu/etias

How to Travel to Hungary

  1. Apply online for ETIAS at travel-europe.europa.eu/etias.
  2. Pay the EUR 7 fee (under 18 and over 70 are exempt).
  3. Wait for your authorisation (usually within minutes).
  4. Travel to Hungary — ETIAS is checked electronically at the border.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport (6+ months validity recommended)
  • Valid ETIAS authorisation linked to your passport
  • Proof of sufficient funds
  • Return/onward travel ticket (may be requested)

Frequently Asked Questions

Do Mexican citizens need a visa for Hungary?

No. Mexican passport holders can enter Hungary visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period. However, from mid-2025 the EU ETIAS (European Travel Information and Authorisation System) is required before travel. ETIAS costs EUR 7 and is valid for 3 years.

What is ETIAS?

ETIAS is the EU's electronic travel authorisation for nationals of visa-exempt countries. It is not a visa — it is a pre-travel check linked to your passport. It is valid for 3 years or until your passport expires, and costs EUR 7.

How long can Mexican citizens stay in Hungary?

Mexican passport holders may stay up to 90 days in any 180-day period across the entire Schengen Area, including Hungary.

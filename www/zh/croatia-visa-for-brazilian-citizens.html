Do Brazilian Citizens Need a Visa for Croatia?

No. Brazilian passport holders can visit Croatia visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period. Croatia joined the Schengen Area in January 2023, so your 90-day allowance now counts across the entire Schengen zone, not just Croatia.

From 2025, Brazilian travellers must register for ETIAS before entering any Schengen country including Croatia. The online application costs €7 and is valid for 3 years.

ETIAS for Croatia

  • Cost: €7
  • Validity: 3 years (or until passport expiry)
  • Apply: online at the official ETIAS website — approval usually within minutes
  • Required: before boarding any flight/ferry to a Schengen country

Border Documents

  • Valid Brazilian passport (min. 3 months validity beyond planned departure)
  • ETIAS authorisation (from 2025)
  • Return or onward travel ticket
  • Proof of accommodation
  • Proof of sufficient funds (~€100/day recommended)
  • Travel/health insurance

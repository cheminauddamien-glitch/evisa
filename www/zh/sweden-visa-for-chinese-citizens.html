Sweden Schengen Visa for Chinese Citizens

Chinese passport holders must apply for a Schengen Short-Stay Visa (Type C) to visit Sweden. The fee is EUR 80 and processing takes approximately 15 working days. Sweden processes Schengen visas through the Swedish Migration Agency (Migrationsverket) and VFS Global application centres worldwide.

Schengen Visa Key Details

  • Cost: EUR 80 (adults); EUR 40 (children 6–11); free (children under 6)
  • Stay: Up to 90 days in any 180-day period in the Schengen Area
  • Processing: 15 working days (standard); up to 45 calendar days peak period
  • Entries: Single, double, or multiple (as granted)
  • Apply: From 6 months before travel, minimum 15 working days before departure

How to Apply for the Sweden Schengen Visa

  1. Book an appointment at the nearest VFS Global application centre or Sweden consulate/embassy.
  2. Complete the Schengen visa application form (online via https://www.migrationsverket.se/English/Private-individuals/Visiting-Sweden.html).
  3. Gather all required documents (see list below).
  4. Attend your appointment, submit documents, pay EUR 80, and provide biometrics (fingerprints and photo) if not enrolled in the last 59 months.
  5. Await the decision — typically 15 working days.
  6. Collect your passport with visa sticker (or receive it by post if opted).

Required Documents

  • Valid passport (at least 3 months beyond intended stay; issued within the last 10 years)
  • Completed Schengen visa application form
  • Passport-size photos (35×45 mm, white background)
  • Travel itinerary and accommodation proof (hotel bookings or invitation letter)
  • Round-trip flight reservations
  • Travel insurance (min. EUR 30,000 coverage, valid across Schengen)
  • Bank statements (last 3 months, showing sufficient funds)
  • Proof of employment, business registration, or student enrollment
  • Leave approval letter (if employed)
  • Biometrics (fingerprints and photo) if not enrolled recently

Do South African Citizens Need a Visa for France?

Yes. South African passport holders must obtain a Schengen Type C visa before travelling to France. Applications are processed at the French Embassy in Pretoria or through VFS Global South Africa centres in Johannesburg, Cape Town, and Durban.

Schengen Visa Requirements

The Schengen visa permits stays of up to 90 days in any 180-day period across all 27 Schengen member states. The non-refundable fee is €80 per adult. Apply at least 3 weeks before travel.

How to Apply

  1. Register at france-visas.gouv.fr and complete the online application form.
  2. Book an appointment at VFS Global or the French Embassy in South Africa.
  3. Attend in person with all required documents and provide biometrics.
  4. Pay the €80 fee and track your application status online.

Required Documents

  • Valid South African passport (min. 2 blank pages)
  • Two recent colour passport photos (35×45 mm, white background)
  • Completed Schengen visa application form
  • Bank statements — last 3 months
  • Travel insurance — min. €30,000 Schengen-wide coverage
  • Confirmed return flight itinerary
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking or host invitation)
  • Employer letter or proof of financial means

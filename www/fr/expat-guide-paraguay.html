Guide Expatriation : Paraguay 2026

Paraguay is Latin America's best-kept secret for expats seeking a low-tax, low-cost destination. The country operates on a territorial tax system (only local income is taxed), permanent residency is obtainable in 3 months, et the cost of living is among the lowest in South America. Asunción is a modern, safe capital with a growing expat community.

🛂 Visa & Options de Résidence

Type de VisaDétails
Permanent Residency (Residencia Permanente)Paraguay's flagship path: deposit $5,500 in a Paraguayan bank + monthly income proof of $1,250 (or pension). Permanent residency issued within 60–90 days. No minimum stay requirement once obtained.
Simple Residency (Residencia Simple)Temporary residency via employment, business or investment. Path to permanent residency.
Jubilado (Retiree)Pour les retraités with a pension of $1,250/mois. Permanent residency. Populaire auprès de Nouth American et European retirees.
Investou VisaInvestment in Paraguayan business or real estate. No strict minimum but typically $70,000+ demonstrated.
Visa-Free EntryUS, EU, UK et many other nationalities: 90 days visa-free. Renouvelable avec une sortie frontalière rapide.

Processus Étape par Étape

  1. Hire a Paraguayan immigration lawyer (budget $500–2,000 total)
  2. Ouvrez un compte bancaire paraguayen et effectuez le dépôt de 5 500 $
  3. Rassemblez les documents : passport, police clearance, birth certificate, proof of income
  4. Apostille all foreign documents et translate to Spanish
  5. Submit application to MIGRACIONES (Immigration Department)
  6. Receive temporary certificate within 2–4 weeks
  7. Obtain permanent residency cedula within 60–90 days
  8. Postulez pour tax residency certificate (for international tax purposes) — optional but valuable
💡 Conseil Pro : Paraguay's permanent residency is one of the fastest et cheapest in the world ($5,500 deposit + lawyer fees). Combined with the territorial tax system (no tax on foreign-source income), it's a powerful tax residency option for international entrepreneurs et investors.

🏥 Santé en Paraguay

Santé Publique

Paraguay's public health system (IPS — Instituto de Previsión Social) covers registered workers et their families. Quality is inconsistent — good in Asunción, very limited in rural areas. Emergency hospitals are available but souvent overcrowded.

Santé Privée

Hôpital privés in Asunción fournit the best care: Hospital Bautista, Centro Médico La Coûta, Hospital Adventista. Quality is acceptable for routine et moderate care, but complex procedures may require travel to Brazil or Argentina. Coûts are very low by international standards.

🛡️ Assurance Santé au Paraguay

Private assurance santé n'est pas requise for residency in Paraguay. Cependant, given the limitations of the local healthcare system for serious conditions, international assurance santé est fortement recommandée.

💡 Conseil Pro : Given that complex medical cases in Paraguay souvent require travel to São Paulo (Brazil) or Buenos Aires (Argentina), make sure your international assurance santé covers treatment in neighbouring countries, not just Paraguay.

🏦 Ouvrir un Compte Bancaire au Paraguay

A Paraguayan bank account est requis(e) for the residency deposit ($5,500) et pour les transactions quotidiennes. The process is relatively straightforward compared to other Latin American countries.

Banques Recommandées

Banco Continental — Paraguay's largest private bank. Bon support en anglais. Recommended for residency applications.
Banco Itaú Paraguay — Brazilian-owned. Strong digital banking, international connections. Populaire auprès de Brazilian expats.
Banco GNB Paraguay — Bon pour account opening. Accepts foreigners with passport only at some branches.
BBVA Paraguay — International bank. Bon pour wire transfers to/from Spain et Latin America.
Banco Nacional de Fomento — Banque d'État. La plus accessible mais services numériques limités.

Documents Requis

  • Passeport valide
  • Justificatif de domicile en Paraguay (rental contract or hotel bill)
  • Police clearance certificate (some banks)
  • Preuve de revenus or purpose of account
  • Dépôt initial ($100–$5,500 depending on account type)

Processus Étape par Étape

  1. Rendez-vous à l'agence bancaire en personne
  2. For residency deposit: ouvrez un fixed-term savings account with $5,500
  3. Standard account opening takes 1–3 jours ouvrables
  4. Recevez votre numéro de compte et l'accès pour les virements
  5. Mobile banking available après account activation
💡 Conseil Pro : Banco Continental est le plus populaire bank for residency purposes among expats. The $5,500 deposit must remain in the account until residency is approved — it earns a small amount of interest in the meantime.

🏠 Acheter un Bien Immobilier au Paraguay

Paraguay has no restrictions on foreign property ownership. The market is extremely affordable — land et properties in Asunción cost a fraction of comparable Latin American cities. The legal process is straightforward.

Options pour les Étrangers

Freehold Purchase
Pleine propriété. Aucune restriction pour les étrangers. Standard pour tous les types de biens.
Agricultural Let
Foreigners can own agricultural let. Paraguay has become a major soy et cattle farming country, with expat farmers from Brazil, Germany et Mennonite communities owning large tracts.
Off-Plan / Development
Growing in Asunción's Villa Moura et Carmelitas neighbourhoods. Lower prices with developer payment plans.

Processus d'Achat

  1. Obtain a RUC (tax ID) at the SET (tax authority)
  2. Hire a Paraguayan notary (escribano) — manages the whole process
  3. Conduct a title search at the General Directouate of Public Registries
  4. Verify property is free of liens et back taxes (IBI)
  5. Signez le/la Escritura Pública (purchase deed) avant the notary
  6. Inscrivez-vous à the Property Registry

Coûts Typiques

ÉlémentCoût

💡 Conseil Pro : Paraguay is one of South America's most affordable property markets. A 2-bedroom apartment in Asunción's Carmelitas neighbourhood costs $80,000–150,000 — comparable areas in Buenos Aires or Santiago are 3–5× more expensive. Land prices in the countryside start at $500/hectare.

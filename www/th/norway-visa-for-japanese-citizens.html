Norway Entry for Japanese Citizens — ETIAS

Japanese passport holders can visit Norway without a traditional visa, but must obtain an ETIAS authorisation before boarding. ETIAS is an electronic pre-travel check, not a visa. Norway is not an EU member but is a full Schengen Area associate. From mid-2025, ETIAS is required for visa-free travellers. ETIAS costs EUR 7 and is valid for 3 years or until passport expiry.

ETIAS Key Details

  • Cost: EUR 7 (applicants aged 18–70; free for under 18 and over 70)
  • Validity: 3 years or until passport expiry (whichever is sooner)
  • Entries: Multiple
  • Stay per visit: Up to 90 days in any 180-day period in the Schengen Area
  • Processing: Usually instant; up to 72 hours in some cases; up to 14 days in rare cases

How to Apply for ETIAS

  1. Visit the official ETIAS website at travel-europe.eu/etias.
  2. Complete the online application form with your passport details.
  3. Pay the EUR 7 fee by card.
  4. Receive ETIAS approval by email — usually within minutes.
  5. ETIAS is linked electronically to your passport. No physical document is issued.

Required Documents for ETIAS

  • Valid passport (must be valid for the duration of your stay)
  • Email address
  • Credit or debit card for EUR 7 fee

ETIAS vs Schengen Visa

ETIAS is not a visa. It is an automated security pre-screening tool for nationals who already enjoy visa-free access to the Schengen Area. Japanese citizens remain visa-free — ETIAS is simply a travel authorisation required before boarding.

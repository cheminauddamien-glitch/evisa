Do Mexican Citizens Need a Visa for Switzerland?

No. Mexican passport holders can enter Switzerland and the entire Schengen Area without a visa for up to 90 days in any 180-day period. No consulate appointment is required.

Switzerland is not an EU member but is a full Schengen Area associate. A Schengen visa issued by Switzerland is valid across all 27 Schengen countries, and vice versa.

From 2025, travellers must obtain ETIAS (European Travel Information and Authorisation System) before departure. The application is entirely online, costs €7, and is valid for 3 years.

ETIAS — How to Apply

  1. Visit etias.com and complete the online application form.
  2. Pay the €7 fee by credit or debit card.
  3. Receive ETIAS authorisation by email — usually within minutes to hours.
  4. ETIAS is electronically linked to your passport. Carry your passport when travelling.

Documents Required at the Border

  • Valid Mexican passport (valid for the full duration of your stay)
  • ETIAS authorisation (from 2025)
  • Return or onward travel ticket
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking or host invitation)
  • Travel/health insurance covering at least €30,000 in the Schengen Area
  • Proof of sufficient funds (~€120 per day of stay)

Schengen 90/180-Day Rule

The 90-day limit applies across the entire Schengen Zone, not just Switzerland. Days spent in France, Germany, Spain, or any other Schengen country count against your Switzerland allowance. Plan accordingly to avoid overstaying.

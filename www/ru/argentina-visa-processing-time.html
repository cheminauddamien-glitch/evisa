Overview of Argentina Visa Processing Times in 2026

Visa-free nationals require no prior processing — entry is granted upon arrival at Ezeiza International Airport or other ports of entry. Immigration processing at the airport typically takes 10–30 minutes. Consular visas for non-exempt nationalities take 5–15 business days to process. Temporary residency applications through the National Directorate of Migration (DNM) can take 2–6 months. Always check current entry requirements at cancilleria.gob.ar before travel, as bilateral agreements may change.

Tips to Speed Up Your Argentina Visa Application

  • Apply well in advance — at least 6–8 weeks before travel for consular visas
  • Ensure all documents are complete, translated (if required), and clearly legible
  • Use the official online portal where available for fastest processing
  • Select priority or express processing if available and your schedule demands it
  • Double-check photo requirements to avoid rejection — see our Visa Photo Requirements guide
  • Track your application status online using your reference number

What Can Cause Delays?

Common causes of Argentina visa processing delays include incomplete document submissions, high application volumes during peak travel seasons, additional security or background checks, requests for supplementary information (often adding 1–3 weeks), and technical issues with online portals. Public holidays in Argentina or in the applicant's country can also pause processing. If your application is taking longer than the standard timeframe, contact the embassy or check the official tracking portal. Pre-Travel Checklist

Once your Argentina visa is approved: confirm entry conditions printed on the visa, ensure your passport remains valid for the full duration of your planned stay, carry printed copies of your visa and booking confirmations, check if onward/return tickets must be presented at immigration, and purchase travel insurance covering medical evacuation.

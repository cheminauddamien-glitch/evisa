Greece Visa Fees 2026: Full Cost Breakdown & Payment Guide

Understand every cost involved in applying for a Greece visa in 2026 — from the official government fee to service charges, children's discounts, and refund policies.

Official Greece Visa Fee Schedule 2026

Fee Schedule — Greece Schengen Visa (type C) / Golden Visa
Schengen short-stay visa (adult)EUR 80
Schengen visa (child 6–11)EUR 40

All fees listed are official government charges. Additional service fees may apply at VFS Global or other authorised application centres. Fees are subject to change; always verify at mfa.gr.

Accepted Payment Methods

Most Greece visa applications accept payment by Visa and MasterCard credit or debit cards through the official online portal. Some embassies and consulates also accept bank transfers, money orders, or cash in local currency at the application centre. Check your local consulate or VFS Global website for the accepted payment options in your country.

Important: never pay visa fees to unofficial third-party agents who claim to offer "faster processing." The official fee at the government portal is always the same regardless of who submits it.

Refund Policy

The Greece visa application fee is non-refundable regardless of outcome. This applies even if:

  • Your visa application is refused
  • You cancel your trip after applying
  • You withdraw your application before a decision is made

To minimise the risk of losing your fee, ensure all documents are complete and correct before submitting. Review the official checklist at mfa.gr.

Money-Saving Tips for Greece Visa Fees 2026

Apply directly on the official government portal to avoid third-party surcharges. Check whether your nationality qualifies for a reduced-fee or visa-free arrangement with Greece. For families, confirm children's fee exemptions or reductions before applying. If applying for multiple Greece visas in the same year, a multiple-entry visa may offer better value than several single-entry fees.

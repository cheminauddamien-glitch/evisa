Mexico eVisa Requirements 2026: Documents, Eligibility & How to Apply

Planning to visit Mexico in 2026? This guide covers every document you need, who is eligible, and the exact steps to apply for a Mexico Visa-free / FMM Tourist Card / Consular Visa.

Overview: Mexico Visa & Entry Requirements 2026

Visa-free for 100+ nationalities including EU, UK, US, Canada, Japan, Australia. The Visa-free / FMM Tourist Card / Consular Visa system makes Mexico highly accessible — apply online at inm.gob.mx.

The official fee is Visa-free for 100+ countries; FMM card USD 30; consular visa USD 36 with a standard processing time of Instant (visa-free + FMM); 3–5 days (consular visa). The permitted stay is up to 180 days (visa-free / tourist card). Entry is Multiple entry (visa-free); single entry (consular visa typical). Currency in use: MXN (Mexican Peso).

Who Is Eligible for a Mexico Visa?

Eligibility: Visa-free for 100+ nationalities; consular visa for others.

Special note: Visa-free for 100+ nationalities including EU, UK, US, Canada, Japan, Australia.

Children under 18 must travel with a valid passport of their own. Minors travelling without both parents may need a notarised parental consent letter. Always verify current rules at the official Mexico immigration authority before travel.

How to Apply: Step-by-Step for Mexico in 2026

  1. Check if you need a visa for Mexico — Visit inm.gob.mx to check your nationality's status. Over 100 nationalities including EU, UK, US, Canada, Japan, and Australia are visa-exempt.
  2. Prepare your travel documents — Bring a valid passport (6+ months validity), return or onward ticket, and accommodation details. The FMM tourist card will be issued at the airport or border.
  3. Complete the FMM tourist card — For air arrivals: fill in the form on the plane or at immigration. For land border crossings: complete the FMM at the INM booth and pay approximately USD 30 in cash.
  4. Pass Mexico immigration — Present your passport, FMM, and any supporting documents to the immigration officer. They will stamp your passport and write your permitted days (up to 180) on the FMM.
  5. Keep your FMM throughout your stay — Store the bottom portion of the FMM in your passport. You must return it when you leave Mexico. Losing it requires a replacement from INM at extra cost.

Apply via the official portal: inm.gob.mx. Avoid third-party agents who charge unnecessary service fees on top of the official government fee.

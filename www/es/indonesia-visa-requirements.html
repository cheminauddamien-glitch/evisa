Indonesia erequisitos de visa 2026: Documents, Eligibility & Cómo solicitar

Planning to visit Indonesia in 2026? This guide covers every document you need, who is eligible, and the exact steps to apply for a Indonesia eVOA (electronic Visa on Arrival).

Overview: Indonesia Visa & requisitos de entrada 2026

Visa-free for ASEAN nationals; eVOA for most other nationalities. The eVOA (electronic Visa on Arrival) system makes Indonesia highly accessible — apply online at molina.imigrasi.go.id.

The official fee is IDR 500,000 (~USD 32) with a standard processing time of 1–2 business days. The permitted stay is up to 30 days (extendable once). Entry is Single entry. Currency in use: IDR (Indonesian Rupiah).

Who Is Eligible for a Indonesia Visa?

Eligibility: 86+ nationalities eligible for eVOA.

Special note: Visa-free for ASEAN nationals; eVOA for most other nationalities.

Children under 18 must travel with a valid passport of their own. Minors travelling without both parents may need a notarised parental consent letter. Always verify current rules at the official Indonesia immigration authority before travel.

Cómo solicitar: Step-by-Step for Indonesia in 2026

  1. Check eVOA eligibility — Visit molina.imigrasi.go.id and confirm your nationality is among the 86+ countries eligible for the Indonesia eVOA.
  2. Gather required documents — Prepare a valid passport (6+ months validity), a passport-style photo, and proof of return/onward travel out of Indonesia within 30 days.
  3. Complete the online application — Fill in the eVOA application form with personal details, travel dates, and Indonesian accommodation address.
  4. Pay the fee — Pay IDR 500,000 (~USD 32) using a Visa or MasterCard. Save your payment receipt and application reference number.
  5. Print your eVOA QR code — Within 1–2 business days you will receive an approval email with a QR code. Print it or save it to your phone and present it at Indonesian immigration alongside your passport.

Apply via the official portal: molina.imigrasi.go.id. Avoid third-party agents who charge unnecessary service fees on top of the official government fee.

Salud y Requisitos de Entrada

  • Medical exam: Required for KITAS (work/family/retirement) — conducted at Indonesian government hospital.
  • Yellow Fever: Certificate required if arriving from endemic area.
  • Vaccinations: No mandatory vaccinations for most travelers.
  • Health insurance: Required for retirement KITAS and recommended for all long-stay visas.
  • Police clearance: SKCK (Surat Keterangan Catatan Kepolisian) from home country required for work KITAS.
  • Biometrics: Collected at immigration office during KITAS processing.

Extension y Estancia Excesiva

  • Visa on Arrival: Extendable once for 30 days at any immigration office. Fee: IDR 500,000. Total maximum: 60 days.
  • Visa-Free Entry: CANNOT be extended. Must exit Indonesia.
  • e-Visa B211A: Extendable up to 4 times for 60 days each. Apply through immigration agent or immigration office.
  • KITAS: Renewable. After 4 consecutive years on KITAS: eligible for KITAP (Permanent Stay Permit).
  • Overstay: Fine of IDR 1,000,000 per day (max IDR 500,000,000). Detention and deportation possible.

Rechazo y Apelaciones

  • Common refusal reasons: Previous overstay, blacklisted, insufficient funds, incomplete documents, security concerns.
  • Appeal: No formal appeal for e-Visa refusals. KITAS refusals can be reconsidered with additional documentation.
  • Reapplication: Can reapply with corrected documentation. Blacklisted individuals must seek clearance from Indonesian immigration.

Visa Photo Requirements 2026 — Size, Background & Specifications by Country

An incorrect passport photo is one of the most common reasons visa applications are rejected. This guide covers the exact specifications required by 15 major destination countries in 2026.

Important: eVisa digital photo uploads must typically be JPEG format, under 1 MB, and taken within the last 6 months. Printed photos must be colour, matte or glossy, not pixelated or edited.

Standard International Visa Photo Specifications

  • Size: 35 mm × 45 mm (most of Europe, Asia, Australia)
  • Background: Plain white or off-white — no shadows, patterns, or objects
  • Face coverage: 70–80% of the frame; head fully visible including top of hair
  • Expression: Neutral, eyes open and looking directly at the camera
  • Glasses: Not permitted for US, UK, Canada, Australia, Schengen since 2022
  • Recency: Taken within the last 6 months (some countries require 3 months)
  • Digital format: JPEG/JPG, 600×800 px minimum, file size under 1 MB

Common Rejection Reasons for Visa Photos

  • Photo taken more than 6 months ago
  • Shadow on the background or face
  • Wearing glasses (prohibited by most countries since 2022)
  • Wearing a hat or head covering (unless for religious purposes)
  • Smiling or mouth open
  • Image too dark, blurry, or digitally altered
  • Wrong dimensions or file size for digital upload
  • Wearing a uniform or camouflage clothing
  • Red-eye or uneven lighting
  • Head tilted or eyes not looking directly at the camera

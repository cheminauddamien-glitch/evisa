Descripción general de Polonia Tiempos de procesamiento de visa 2026

Knowing how long your Poland visa application will take is critical for planning your travel itinerary. In 2026, the standard processing time for a Poland Schengen short-stay visa is 15 working days (up to 45 days in complex cases). However, this timeline begins only after the consulate has formally accepted your complete application — incomplete submissions are not processed until all deficiencies are resolved, which effectively pauses the clock.

Processing time is counted in working days and excludes public holidays in Poland and in your country of application. During peak travel seasons — particularly summer (June–August) and major European holiday periods — appointment availability and processing times may be longer due to higher application volumes. Planning ahead by applying 4–6 weeks before travel is strongly recommended.

Cronograma detallado de procesamiento

A typical Polonia visa application journey follows these stages: booking and attending a VFS Global appointment; biometric data collection (fingerprints and photograph, for first-time applicants); dispatch of the application file to the Poland consulate; background and security checks; consular review and decision; and finally return of the passport with a visa sticker or a refusal letter. For straightforward applications from low-risk applicants, the decision is often made within 5–10 working days. Complex cases — those involving previous refusals, criminal history, or incomplete documents — may take up to 45 calendar days as permitted under the Schengen Visa Code.

How to Track Your Poland Visa Application

After submitting your Poland visa application at a VFS Global visa application centre, you will receive a unique reference number. Use this number on the VFS Global online tracking portal to monitor your application status in real time. Status updates include: “Application Submitted”, “Under Review”, “Additional Documents Requested”, “Decision Made”, and “Passport Ready for Collection”. Keep your email and mobile number current in your application profile so you receive timely notifications.

Consejos para evitar retrasos

  • Submit a complete application — a single missing document can pause processing entirely
  • Apply 4–6 weeks before travel to allow buffer time for delays or additional requests
  • Ensure your passport photo meets the exact 35×45mm biometric specification
  • Use the official https://www.gov.pl/web/diplomacy website or VFS Global portal — avoid third-party intermediaries
  • Keep your email inbox monitored and check the spam folder regularly
  • Respond immediately to any requests for additional information from the Polonia consulate

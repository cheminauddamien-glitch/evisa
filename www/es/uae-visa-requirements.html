UAE erequisitos de visa 2026: Documents, Eligibility & Cómo solicitar

Planning to visit UAE in 2026? This guide covers every document you need, who is eligible, and the exact steps to apply for a UAE eVisa / Tourist Visa.

Overview: UAE Visa & requisitos de entrada 2026

90 days visa-free for EU, UK, US, Australia and many other nationalities. The eVisa / Tourist Visa system makes UAE highly accessible — apply online at icp.gov.ae.

The official fee is AED 250–350 (~USD 68–95) for non-EU; 90-day visa-free for EU with a standard processing time of 3–5 business days. The permitted stay is up to 90 days (EU visa-free); 30/60 days (eVisa). Entry is Single or multiple entry. Currency in use: AED (UAE Dirham).

Who Is Eligible for a UAE Visa?

Eligibility: Visa-free for 50+ nationalities; eVisa for others.

Special note: 90 days visa-free for EU, UK, US, Australia and many other nationalities.

Children under 18 must travel with a valid passport of their own. Minors travelling without both parents may need a notarised parental consent letter. Always verify current rules at the official UAE immigration authority before travel.

Cómo solicitar: Step-by-Step for UAE in 2026

  1. Determine if you need a visa — Check the UAE Federal Authority for Identity and Citizenship (icp.gov.ae) to confirm whether your nationality requires a visa. EU, UK, US, and Australian citizens can enter visa-free for 90 days.
  2. Prepare required documents — Gather a passport valid for 6+ months with 2 blank pages, a recent passport photo, confirmed return ticket, hotel booking, 3 months bank statements, and optional travel insurance.
  3. Submit your application — Apply online at icp.gov.ae, through the UAE embassy in your country, or via Emirates/Etihad airline if you are flying with them.
  4. Pay the visa fee — Pay AED 250–650 (~USD 68–177) depending on the visa type using Visa or MasterCard. Some agents accept local payment methods.
  5. Receive and review your visa — Within 3–5 business days you will receive your UAE visa by email. Print it and check all details match your passport before travel.

Apply via the official portal: icp.gov.ae. Avoid third-party agents who charge unnecessary service fees on top of the official government fee.

Salud y Requisitos de Entrada

  • Medical fitness test: Required for all residence visa holders (employment, family, student). Includes blood test (HIV, Hepatitis B/C, syphilis, tuberculosis) and chest X-ray. Conducted at SEHA health centers or approved clinics. Cost: AED 250-500.
  • Failing medical: Positive results for HIV, Hepatitis B/C, or active tuberculosis may result in visa denial and deportation (though policies have been relaxed for Hepatitis B in some cases).
  • Health insurance: Mandatory for all residents in Dubai (ISAHD) and Abu Dhabi (HAAD). Employer must provide health insurance for employees.
  • Vaccinations: No mandatory vaccinations for entry.
  • Police clearance: Not routinely required for visa applications. May be required for certain employment or government positions.
  • Emirates ID: Biometric Emirates ID card required for all residents. Includes fingerprints and iris scan. Applied for after entry on residence visa.

Extension y Estancia Excesiva

  • Visa on Arrival (30 days): Extendable for 30 days at a GDRFA office or online. Fee: AED 600-1,000. Can extend twice for a total of 90 days.
  • Tourist Visa: Extendable twice (30 days each). Total maximum stay varies by visa type.
  • Employment Visa: Renewed by employer through MOHRE. Must maintain valid Emirates ID and medical fitness.
  • Golden Visa: Automatically renewable for 10 years as long as qualifying conditions are maintained.
  • Grace period: 30-day grace period after visa expiry or cancellation to either leave the UAE or change status.
  • Overstay: Fine of AED 50 per day (first 6 months), increasing to AED 100/day thereafter. Maximum fine before legal action.

Rechazo y Apelaciones

  • Common refusal reasons: Previous overstay or absconding record, failed medical fitness test, security concerns, incomplete documentation, previous deportation.
  • Appeal: No formal public appeal process for tourist visa refusals. Employment visa denials can be challenged through MOHRE. Medical fitness failures may allow re-testing.
  • Blacklist: Immigration blacklist can result from absconding from employer, criminal record, or deportation. Removal requires employer or legal intervention.
  • Reapplication: For tourist visas, reapply with corrected documentation. Entry bans from overstay or absconding require clearance from GDRFA.

How to Extend Your Hong Kong Visa in 2026

Extending your stay in Hong Kong requires applying through the official immigration authority before your current visa or permitted stay expires. This guide covers the extension process, fees, documents required and the consequences of overstaying.

Extension at a Glance

Apply before your current visa expires to remain in legal status throughout the process.

Extension TypeDurationFeeWhere to ApplyProcessing Time
Extension of Stay Usually in multiples of weeks up to 12 months HKD 230 (~USD 30) Hong Kong Immigration Department Same day at Immigration Tower (Wan Chai)

Can You Extend Your Hong Kong Visa?

Yes, in most cases tourists can request a stay extension through Hong Kong Immigration Department. Extensions are typically processed within Same day at Immigration Tower (Wan Chai). The standard fee is HKD 230 (~USD 30). You must apply before your current authorised stay expires — applying in time ensures you remain in lawful status during processing.

Requirements & Documents

  • Valid passport (minimum 6 months validity beyond requested stay)
  • Current Hong Kong visa or entry stamp (not yet expired)
  • Completed extension application form (available from Hong Kong Immigration Department)
  • Proof of sufficient funds (recent bank statement)
  • Proof of accommodation in Hong Kong
  • Onward or return travel booking
  • Proof of reason for extension (if required)
  • Application fee: HKD 230 (~USD 30)

Step-by-Step Extension Process

  1. Gather your documents
    Prepare passport, bank statements, accommodation proof, and a completed application form before visiting the immigration office.
  2. Visit or apply online at Hong Kong Immigration Department
    Submit your extension application at https://www.immd.gov.hk/eng/index.html or at a local immigration office.
  3. Pay the fee
    The current standard fee is HKD 230 (~USD 30). Payment is usually required at the time of application.
  4. Wait for processing
    Processing typically takes Same day at Immigration Tower (Wan Chai). Do not depart Hong Kong while your application is pending if you have not been granted a bridging status.
  5. Receive your extension
    Your new authorised stay will be noted in your passport or issued as an official document. Keep a copy for your records.

Overstay Consequences — Important!

Warning: Overstaying your visa or authorised stay in Hong Kong can have serious consequences.

Overstaying in Hong Kong without valid permission is a criminal offence. Overstayers may be detained and prosecuted. This can result in a ban from future entry to Hong Kong.

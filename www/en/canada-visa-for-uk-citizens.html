Canada eTA for UK Citizens — What You Need to Know

UK passport holders are visa-exempt for Canada but must obtain an Electronic Travel Authorisation (eTA) before flying to Canada. The eTA is a simple online process, costs CAD 7, and is typically approved within minutes. It is valid for 5 years or until passport expiry (whichever comes first) and allows multiple trips.

Note: An eTA is only required for air travel. UK citizens arriving by land or sea do not need an eTA (a valid passport is sufficient).

How to Apply for a Canadian eTA in 2026

  1. Visit the official site: canada.ca/eTA.
  2. Have your valid UK passport, email address, and a credit/debit card ready.
  3. Fill in the online form and pay CAD 7.
  4. Receive your eTA approval email — usually within minutes.
  5. Your eTA is linked electronically to your passport; no need to print anything.

What to Bring When Entering Canada

  • Valid UK passport (with approved eTA linked)
  • Return or onward ticket
  • Proof of accommodation in Canada
  • Sufficient funds for your stay
  • Travel/health insurance (recommended)

Staying Longer or Moving to Canada

The eTA allows visits of up to 6 months at a time. For longer stays, study, or work, you must apply for the appropriate Canadian permit or visa through IRCC (Immigration, Refugees and Citizenship Canada) before or during your stay.

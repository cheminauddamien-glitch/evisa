How to Apply for an eVisa in 2026 — Step-by-Step Guide

An eVisa (electronic visa) lets you apply online before travelling — no embassy visit required. This guide walks you through every step, from finding the official portal to arriving at border control.

Good to know: Over 190 countries now offer some form of electronic travel authorisation. Most eVisas are approved within 72 hours and cost under $50 USD.

The 8 Steps to Apply for an eVisa

  1. Step 1 — Find the official eVisa portal. Visit the destination country's government immigration website. Bookmark the official URL and ignore fee-charging third-party sites for government visas.
  2. Step 2 — Create an account. Register with a valid email. Store login credentials safely — you'll need them to check status and download your visa.
  3. Step 3 — Fill in the application form. Enter your full name (exactly as in your passport), nationality, date of birth, travel dates, and accommodation address. Double-check before submitting.
  4. Step 4 — Upload required documents. Typically: passport bio page scan, recent passport photo, and supporting documents (return ticket, hotel booking, travel insurance).
  5. Step 5 — Pay the visa fee. Most eVisas accept Visa, Mastercard, or PayPal. Fees are usually non-refundable. Save the payment receipt.
  6. Step 6 — Wait for approval. Processing ranges from 30 minutes (Turkey, Bahrain) to 10 business days (India, Sri Lanka). Check your spam folder daily.
  7. Step 7 — Download your eVisa. Save the PDF approval. Print two copies and also store a digital backup in cloud storage or email.
  8. Step 8 — Travel and present your eVisa. Show your eVisa confirmation and passport at the border. The officer will verify and stamp your entry.

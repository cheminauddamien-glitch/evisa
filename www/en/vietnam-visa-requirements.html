Vietnam eVisa Requirements 2026: Documents, Eligibility & How to Apply

Planning to visit Vietnam in 2026? This guide covers every document you need, who is eligible, and the exact steps to apply for a Vietnam eVisa.

Overview: Vietnam Visa & Entry Requirements 2026

45-day visa-free for France, Germany, UK and 10 other nationalities. The eVisa system makes Vietnam highly accessible — apply online at evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn.

The official fee is USD 25 with a standard processing time of 3 business days. The permitted stay is up to 90 days. Entry is Single or multiple entry. Currency in use: VND (Vietnamese Dong).

Who Is Eligible for a Vietnam Visa?

Eligibility: All nationalities.

Special note: 45-day visa-free for France, Germany, UK and 10 other nationalities.

Children under 18 must travel with a valid passport of their own. Minors travelling without both parents may need a notarised parental consent letter. Always verify current rules at the official Vietnam immigration authority before travel.

How to Apply: Step-by-Step for Vietnam in 2026

  1. Check eligibility — Visit evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn and verify whether your nationality qualifies for visa-free entry or must apply for a 90-day eVisa.
  2. Prepare your documents — Gather a valid passport (6+ months validity), a recent digital passport photo with plain background, and a clear scan of your passport bio-data page.
  3. Complete the online application — Fill in the eVisa form with your personal details, travel dates, and accommodation address. Double-check all information matches your passport exactly.
  4. Pay the fee — Pay the USD 25 non-refundable fee using Visa or MasterCard. Save your application receipt number for tracking.
  5. Download and print your eVisa — Within 3 business days you will receive an approval email. Download the PDF eVisa and print a copy to present at the Vietnamese port of entry alongside your passport.

Apply via the official portal: evisa.xuatnhapcanh.gov.vn. Avoid third-party agents who charge unnecessary service fees on top of the official government fee.

Health & Character Requirements

  • Medical exam: Required for work permit applicants. Must be performed at a Vietnamese government-designated hospital.
  • Vaccinations: Yellow Fever certificate required if arriving from endemic area. No other mandatory vaccinations.
  • Health insurance: Not mandatory for tourists but recommended. Required for work permit and temporary residence card holders.
  • Police clearance: Required for work permit applications — from home country, apostilled and translated into Vietnamese.
  • Biometrics: Fingerprints and photo may be collected at immigration checkpoints.

Visa Extension & Renewal

  • e-Visa (90 days): Cannot be extended. Must exit and obtain a new e-Visa.
  • Visa-free entry: Cannot be extended. Must exit Vietnam for at least 30 days before re-entering visa-free (for most nationalities).
  • Tourist/business visa: Can be extended through a Vietnamese travel agency or immigration office. Extensions in 30-day increments. Cost: USD 50-100 per extension.
  • Work permit / TRC: Renewable through employer.
  • Overstay: Fine of USD 25 per day (approximately). Deportation and entry ban possible for serious overstays.

Visa Refusal & Appeals

  • Common refusal reasons: Incorrect passport details on application, previously blacklisted, security concerns, incomplete documents.
  • Appeal: No formal appeal process. Reapply with corrected information.
  • Reapplication: Can reapply immediately. Ensure all details match passport exactly.

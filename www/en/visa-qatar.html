Introduction

Qatar is one of the wealthiest nations in the world, known for Hamad International Airport (one of the world's best), ultra-luxury tourism, and the 2022 FIFA World Cup legacy. Qatar has implemented a progressive, open visa policy — over 95 nationalities can enter visa-free or with a visa on arrival. The eVisa is available for all other nationalities via visa.moi.gov.qa.

Tourist & Short Stay

  • Visa-Free (30 days): Citizens of 97+ nationalities including USA, UK, EU countries, Canada, Australia, Japan, South Korea, Singapore, and GCC members enter Qatar without a visa for 30 days. Extension possible.
  • Visa on Arrival (Free): Available for citizens of select countries not covered by visa-waiver — free at DOH airport. 30-day stay.
  • eVisa — QAR 100 + QAR 10 insurance (~USD 30 total): For nationalities not covered by visa-free or VoA programs. Apply at visa.moi.gov.qa. Processing: instant–3 business days. Valid 30 days, single or multiple entry.
  • Stopover Visa (96 hours, free): Qatar Airways passengers transiting through Doha can apply for a free 96-hour stopover visa, including hotel packages. Apply at qatarairways.com.
  • Extension: Apply at Ministry of Interior (moi.gov.qa). Initial 30-day tourist stay can be extended up to 6 months total.

Business Visa

Business visits are permitted on the tourist visa/VoA for most nationalities. For long-term business, company registration with the Ministry of Commerce and Industry (MOCI) and a residency permit are required. Qatar offers 100% foreign ownership in many sectors and free zone opportunities (Qatar Financial Centre, Manateq free zones).

Work & Long-Stay Visas

  • Work Residence Permit (QID): Employer-sponsored; employer files with the Ministry of Labour. Worker receives a Qatar ID (QID) upon arrival with medical and biometric checks. Labour Law No. 14 of 2004 governs employment. Reform: no-objection certificate (NOC) for job changes largely abolished in 2020.
  • Investor/Business Visa: For investors in Qatari companies or projects. Qatar Investment Authority (QIA) and MOCI manage investor visas.
  • Freelance Permit: Qatar introduced freelance permits in 2020 for self-employed professionals in specific fields.

Student Visa

Students at Qatar Foundation universities (Education City) or Qatar University apply through their institution. Work permission (part-time) may be granted by the employer/institution. Student residence permit issued for the duration of studies.

Airport Transit

Hamad International Airport (DOH) is a major global hub. Most nationalities may transit without a visa. Qatar Airways passengers eligible for the 96-hour Stopover Visa apply at qatarairways.com for a free hotel and city tour package. Landside transit (leaving airport) requires a valid Qatar visa or VoA/visa-free eligibility.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport (6 months validity)
  • eVisa approval or VoA eligibility
  • Return or onward ticket
  • Hotel/accommodation booking confirmation
  • Proof of sufficient funds
  • Travel insurance (included in eVisa fee)
  • For work visa: employer NOC, medical examination, police clearance

How to Apply

  1. Check your eligibility at visa.moi.gov.qa — visa-free, VoA, or eVisa required.
  2. For eVisa: apply at visa.moi.gov.qa. Fill in details, upload passport scan, pay QAR 110. Receive approval by email (instant–3 days).
  3. For Stopover Visa: apply at qatarairways.com if flying Qatar Airways through DOH.
  4. On arrival at DOH: present passport (and eVisa if applicable) at immigration.
  5. Collect QID on arrival if on a work visa or long-stay permit.

Who Is Visa-Exempt?

97+ nationalities are visa-free including: USA, UK, all EU/Schengen countries, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, South Korea, Singapore, Malaysia, all GCC members (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Bahrain, Oman). Nationalities requiring eVisa include most of South Asia, Africa, Central Asia. Check the full list at visa.moi.gov.qa.

Always verify current visa rules on official government sites before travel.

🏥 Health & Entry Requirements for Qatar

Medical exam mandatory for work/residence visa: HIV, Hepatitis B/C, TB, chest X-ray. Must be done at approved medical commission. No vaccinations required for tourists. Health insurance provided by employer for workers.

🕑 Visa Extension & Overstay Rules in Qatar

Tourist visa extendable once for 30 days at MOI. Overstay: QAR 200/day fine. Significant overstay: detention, deportation, entry ban. Exit permit system abolished in 2020 for most workers.

❌ Visa Refusal & Appeals for Qatar

May occur for: certain nationalities, security screening, previous violations. No formal appeal for tourist visa. Work visa depends on employer sponsorship and quota.

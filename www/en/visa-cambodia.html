Introduction

Cambodia is a Southeast Asian nation known for the majestic Angkor Wat temple complex (the world's largest religious monument), the colonial city of Phnom Penh, the emerald beaches of Sihanoukville and Koh Rong, and the heartbreaking history of the Khmer Rouge era. Main airports are Phnom Penh International (PNH) and Siem Reap (REP). The General Department of Immigration manages entry and visa matters.

Cambodia is one of Southeast Asia's most accessible destinations for tourists: almost all nationalities can obtain a Tourist Visa on Arrival or eVisa easily. ASEAN nationals can enter visa-free. Cambodia's eVisa system at evisa.gov.kh is simple and efficient.

Tourist Visa — eVisa & Visa on Arrival

Most nationalities can obtain a Tourist Visa (T-visa) via:

  • eVisa: apply online at evisa.gov.kh at least 3 days before travel. Fee: USD $30. Valid for 30 days, single entry. Accepted at Phnom Penh, Siem Reap airports and some land border crossings.
  • Visa on Arrival: available at international airports (Phnom Penh, Siem Reap) and major land border crossings. Fee: USD $30 for tourist. Bring 1 passport photo and US dollars cash. Valid for 30 days, single entry.

The tourist visa can be extended once for an additional 30 days at the General Department of Immigration in Phnom Penh (fee: USD $30). For longer stays, an Ordinary Visa (E-visa) allows extensions for business or retirement purposes.

Business / Ordinary Visa (E-visa)

The Ordinary Visa (E-visa) is for business, employment, and long-stay purposes. Available on arrival (fee: USD $35) or via the eVisa system. The E-visa must be extended within Cambodia at the Immigration Department. Long-term E-visas can be extended monthly (USD $285/month + fee for 12 months) or for 6–12 months.

Work & Long-Stay

  • Ordinary Visa (E-visa) extensions — the most common long-term stay option; extended at the Immigration Department, often through a registered guesthouse or agency.
  • Business Visa — for foreign investors and business owners; valid 1 year with multiple entry.
  • Retirement Visa — Cambodia has no formal retirement visa but retirees typically stay on long-term E-visa extensions.

Student Visa

International students typically enter on a Tourist or Ordinary Visa and obtain an E-visa extension valid for the duration of their studies. A formal student visa does not exist as a separate category. Required: acceptance letter from a Cambodian institution.

Airport Transit

Transiting through Phnom Penh or Siem Reap airports in the international zone without clearing immigration generally does not require a visa. If clearing immigration, a tourist visa or eVisa is required.

Who Is Visa-Free?

ASEAN member citizens (Malaysia, Indonesia, Thailand, Vietnam, Philippines, Singapore, Laos, Myanmar, Brunei) receive visa-free entry to Cambodia for 14–30 days. All other nationalities can get a Tourist Visa on Arrival or eVisa. Check immigration.gov.kh for the current list.

Always verify current visa rules on official government sites before travel.

🏥 Health & Entry Requirements for Cambodia

Cambodia does not require mandatory vaccinations for most travelers. A Yellow Fever vaccination certificate is required for travelers from endemic countries. Strongly recommended vaccinations include hepatitis A, hepatitis B, typhoid, Japanese encephalitis, and rabies. Malaria prophylaxis is advised for rural and forested areas, especially Mondulkiri and Ratanakiri provinces. No medical examination is required for tourist visas. Work permit applicants must undergo a medical check at a Cambodian-approved clinic. Health insurance is strongly recommended as medical facilities in Cambodia are limited, and serious cases require evacuation to Bangkok or Singapore. Police clearance certificates are required for work permits and long-term business visa extensions. Biometric data (fingerprints and photograph) is collected at Phnom Penh International Airport and Siem Reap International Airport on arrival. Dengue fever is endemic — travelers should take mosquito precautions.

🕑 Visa Extension & Overstay Rules in Cambodia

Tourist visas (T-class) can be extended once for 30 additional days at the General Department of Immigration in Phnom Penh. The extension fee is approximately USD 45. After that, you must leave or switch to an E-class visa. Business visas (E-class) can be extended for 1, 3, 6, or 12 months, making them the preferred option for long-term stays. Overstay penalties are USD 10 per day for the first 30 days of overstay, then potential deportation and entry bans of 1-3 years. Cambodia is generally flexible about visa issues and agents can often assist with extensions. However, recent enforcement has increased. Working without a proper work permit carries fines and deportation. The General Department of Immigration occasionally conducts sweeps in Phnom Penh and Siem Reap targeting overstayers.

❌ Visa Refusal & Appeals for Cambodia

Cambodia rarely refuses tourist visa applications or visas on arrival. Refusals may occur for: nationals of certain restricted countries, criminal records, previous deportations from Cambodia, national security concerns, or expired/invalid passports. Port-of-entry refusals result in return on the next flight. eVisa refusals are communicated online with limited explanation. Work permit refusals may be due to insufficient documentation or employer non-compliance. There is no formal appeals process for tourist visa refusals. For work permits, the employer can resubmit with corrected documentation. Engaging a local immigration agent or lawyer is common practice in Cambodia for resolving visa issues. Reapplication is possible immediately. Cambodia's immigration system is generally accommodating, especially for tourism-oriented visas.

How to get the Cambodia visa?

  1. Check whether you need a visa. Confirm your nationality's requirement for Cambodia in the table above (visa-free, eVisa, visa on arrival or consular visa).
  2. Choose the right visa type for your trip — tourism, business, transit, work or study.
  3. Prepare your documents: a passport valid at least 6 months, a recent photo, proof of accommodation and onward travel, and sufficient funds.
  4. Apply online (eVisa) or at the nearest Cambodia embassy/consulate, and pay the visa fee.
  5. Receive your visa and print or save the approval before you travel to Cambodia.

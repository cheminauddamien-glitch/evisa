China Visa-Free Entry for French Citizens

French passport holders benefit from visa-free entry to China for up to 15 days under the 2023–2025 bilateral agreement. For longer stays, a free eVisa (up to 30 days) is available at evisa.mfa.gov.cn.

Visa-Free & eVisa Details

  • Visa-free: 15 days, no application required
  • eVisa: Free, up to 30 days — apply at evisa.mfa.gov.cn
  • eVisa processing: ~4 business days
  • Entry: Most international airports and ports

How to Apply

  1. For short stays (up to 15 days): No advance application needed — present your valid passport at the immigration counter.
  2. For stays up to 30 days: Visit evisa.mfa.gov.cn to apply for a free eVisa.
  3. Complete the online form with passport details, travel dates, and upload a passport photo.
  4. Receive eVisa approval by email within 4 business days.
  5. Print and carry the eVisa approval letter alongside your passport.

Required Documents

  • Valid passport — minimum 6 months validity
  • Passport photo (for eVisa application)
  • Return or onward flight ticket
  • Hotel booking or accommodation address

Cost & Validity for French Travellers

French citizens pay no visa fee to enter China for tourism. The permitted stay is 15 days visa-free; 30 days eVisa, granted automatically on arrival with a valid passport. Because entry is visa-free, there is no application form, no embassy appointment and no processing wait — instant at border (visa-free) / 4 business days (evisa).

Length of Stay & Overstaying China

The standard tourist stay for French citizens is 15 days visa-free; 30 days eVisa. Overstaying this limit in China can lead to fines, deportation and a temporary re-entry ban, so track your permitted days carefully. If you need longer, enquire about an extension or a different visa category with China’s immigration authority before your authorised stay expires.

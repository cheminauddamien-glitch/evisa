Do Japanese Citizens Need a Visa for France?

No. Japanese passport holders can visit France and all 27 Schengen countries visa-free for up to 90 days in any 180-day period — perfect for exploring Paris and the French regions.

From 2025, Japanese travellers must obtain ETIAS before entering the Schengen Area. The online application costs €7 and is valid for 3 years.

Schengen Visa Requirements for Japanese Citizens

No visa is needed, but border officials may ask for: a valid Japanese passport (min. 3 months validity beyond departure), ETIAS authorisation, return or onward ticket, proof of accommodation, and travel insurance with at least €30,000 coverage.

How to Apply (ETIAS)

  1. Apply at the official ETIAS website before travel.
  2. Pay €7 — authorisation is usually issued within minutes.
  3. ETIAS is electronically linked to your Japanese passport.
  4. Valid for 3 years or until passport expiry, whichever comes first.

Required Documents at the Border

  • Valid Japanese passport
  • ETIAS authorisation (from 2025)
  • Return or onward flight ticket
  • Proof of accommodation
  • Travel/health insurance (min. €30,000)
  • Proof of sufficient funds

Switzerland eVisa Requirements 2026: Documents, Eligibility & How to Apply

Planning to visit Switzerland in 2026? This guide covers every document you need, who is eligible, and the exact steps to apply for a Switzerland Schengen Visa (type C).

Overview: Switzerland Visa & Entry Requirements 2026

As a Schengen member, Switzerland issues Type C short-stay visas valid throughout all 27 Schengen states. Non-EU nationals should apply through the Switzerland embassy or an authorised VFS Global application centre in their country of residence. EU, EEA, UK, US, Canadian, and Australian citizens enjoy visa-free access.

The official fee is EUR 80 (embassy fee for Schengen) with a standard processing time of 15 working days. The permitted stay is up to 90 days within 180-day period (Schengen). Entry is Single, double or multiple entry. Currency in use: CHF (Swiss Franc).

Who Is Eligible for a Switzerland Visa?

Eligibility: Non-EU/EEA nationals may need Schengen visa via Swiss embassy.

Special note: Visa-free for EU/EEA, UK, US, Canada, Australia, Japan and many others.

Children under 18 must travel with a valid passport of their own. Minors travelling without both parents may need a notarised parental consent letter. Always verify current rules at the official Switzerland immigration authority before travel.

How to Apply: Step-by-Step for Switzerland in 2026

  1. Check visa requirements at eda.admin.ch — Confirm whether your nationality needs a Schengen visa for Switzerland. EU, EEA, UK, US, Canadian, and Australian citizens enter visa-free.
  2. Gather all required documents — Prepare passport (10 years max age, 3+ months validity), 2 ICAO photos, insurance (EUR 30,000+), flight booking, hotel confirmation, bank statements (EUR 100/day minimum), and employment proof.
  3. Book your appointment — Schedule at the nearest Swiss embassy, VFS Global or TLScontact centre. First-time Schengen applicants must apply in person for biometrics.
  4. Attend appointment and submit application — Arrive on time with all original documents and photocopies. Submit biometrics and pay the EUR 80 fee plus service charges.
  5. Await decision and collect passport — Wait 15 working days (longer in peak seasons). Collect your passport from the application centre when notified.

Apply via the official portal: eda.admin.ch. Avoid third-party agents who charge unnecessary service fees on top of the official government fee.

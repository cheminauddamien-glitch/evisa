Do Canadian Citizens Need a Visa for Colombia?

Canadian passport holders enjoy visa-free access to Colombia. You may stay for up to 90 days per entry (extendable once for another 90 days, up to a maximum of 180 days per year) for tourism or short business visits. No advance visa or pre-travel authorisation is required — simply arrive with a valid passport.

Requirements for Canadian Citizens

  • Valid passport (6+ months validity recommended beyond your planned return date)
  • Return or onward travel ticket
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel booking or host address)
  • Proof of sufficient funds for your stay (credit card or equivalent cash)
  • Travel health insurance (recommended; yellow fever vaccination recommended if visiting Amazon region)

How to Apply

No visa application is required for stays up to 90 days.

  1. Book flights and accommodation — ensure your passport is valid for the duration of your trip.
  2. Complete the Check-Mig form — Colombia requires travellers to fill out the online Check-Mig migration form before arrival at apps.migracioncolombia.gov.co.
  3. Arrive at a Colombian port of entry — present your passport and Check-Mig confirmation to the immigration officer.
  4. Receive your entry stamp — the officer will stamp your passport with the permitted stay (up to 90 days).

To extend your stay beyond 90 days, visit a Migraciones Colombia office before your initial period expires.

Tips for Canadian Travellers to Colombia

  • Complete the Check-Mig form online before your flight — it is mandatory for most arrivals.
  • The 90-day entry stamp is at the discretion of the immigration officer; carry proof of sufficient funds.
  • Colombia operates on COP (Colombian Peso); carry some local cash for areas outside major cities.
  • If you plan to visit the Amazon or certain rural regions, a yellow fever vaccination is strongly recommended.
  • Extensions (prórrogas) of up to 90 additional days can be requested at any Migraciones Colombia office.

Do Philippine Citizens Need a Visa for Costa Rica?

Philippine passport holders require a Costa Rican visa to enter the country. Applications must be submitted in person or by mail at the nearest Costa Rican Embassy or Consulate. Fees vary by consulate but are typically in the range of USD 30–100. Processing usually takes 5–15 business days. Check the official Costa Rican Ministry of Foreign Affairs website at rree.go.cr for the consulate nearest you.

Requirements for Philippine Citizens

  • Valid passport (at least 6 months validity beyond the intended stay; at least 2 blank pages)
  • Completed and signed Costa Rica visa application form
  • Recent passport-size photograph (white background)
  • Bank statements for the last 3–6 months (proof of sufficient funds, approximately USD 100/week)
  • Round-trip flight itinerary
  • Proof of accommodation (hotel reservation or host invitation letter)
  • Employment letter, proof of business ownership, or proof of student enrolment
  • Visa fee (amount varies by consulate; ask your nearest Costa Rican mission for details)
  • Travel health insurance covering the duration of stay in Costa Rica

How to Apply

  1. Locate the nearest Costa Rican Embassy or Consulate via rree.go.cr.
  2. Download the visa application form from the consulate's website or collect it in person.
  3. Gather all required documents — see the list above.
  4. Submit the application — in person or by mail (check with your consulate for accepted methods).
  5. Pay the visa fee — amount varies by consulate, typically USD 30–100.
  6. Wait for the decision — processing takes 5–15 business days. The consulate will contact you.
  7. Receive your visa — if approved, the visa sticker is affixed to your passport and returned to you.

Official website: rree.go.cr

Tips for Philippine Travellers to Costa Rica

  • Contact your nearest Costa Rican consulate well in advance — processing times can vary significantly.
  • Ensure your travel insurance policy explicitly covers Costa Rica.
  • Costa Rica requires proof of onward/return travel — carry your return ticket confirmation.
  • The official currency is CRC (Costa Rican Colón); USD is widely accepted but carry local currency for rural areas.
  • Vaccinations for Hepatitis A and Typhoid are recommended for Costa Rica travellers.

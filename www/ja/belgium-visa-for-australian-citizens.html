Does a Australian Citizen Need a Visa for Belgium?

Australian passport holders do not need a Schengen visa to visit Belgium. However, since the ETIAS system launched, travellers must obtain ETIAS (European Travel Information and Authorisation System) before boarding. The fee is EUR 7 and approval is usually instant.

ETIAS Key Details

  • Cost: EUR 7
  • Validity: 3 years (or until passport expiry, whichever comes first)
  • Entries: Multiple
  • Stay: Up to 90 days in any 180-day period across the Schengen Area (including Belgium)
  • Processing: Usually instant; up to 96 hours in some cases
  • Required: Before boarding — airlines check ETIAS at check-in

How to Apply for ETIAS

  1. Visit travel-europe.europa.eu/etias.
  2. Complete the online application form with your passport details.
  3. Pay the EUR 7 fee by credit or debit card.
  4. Receive your ETIAS authorisation by email (usually within minutes).
  5. Travel to Belgium — present your passport at border control. ETIAS is electronically linked.

Documents Required for ETIAS

  • Valid passport with at least 3 months validity beyond your intended stay
  • Email address for ETIAS confirmation
  • Credit or debit card for the EUR 7 fee
  • Return or onward travel ticket (recommended)
  • Travel/health insurance (recommended)

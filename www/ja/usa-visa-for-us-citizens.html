US Citizens and US Entry — No Visa Required

As a US citizen, you have an absolute right to enter the United States. No visa, ESTA, or other travel authorisation is required. You are only required to present a valid US passport (or other accepted document) when returning from a trip abroad.

Returning to the USA from Abroad

When returning to the USA after international travel, US citizens must present their US passport at the port of entry. US passport cards are accepted at land and sea crossings but are not valid for international air travel. If your passport has expired abroad, the US Embassy can issue an emergency travel document.

Living and Working in the USA as a US Citizen

US citizens have unrestricted rights to live, work, and study anywhere in the United States. If you are interested in becoming a US citizen through naturalisation, contact USCIS (uscis.gov).

Travelling to Other Countries from the USA

The US passport is one of the world's most powerful travel documents, granting visa-free or visa-on-arrival access to over 180 countries. See our US passport visa-free countries guide for a complete list.

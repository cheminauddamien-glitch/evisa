🛂 ビザ・在留資格オプション

ビザの種類詳細
Pensionado (Retiree Visa)Requires a guaranteed lifetime pension of $1,000/month from a government or private source. Permanent residency. Must spend 183 days/year in Costa Rica to maintain status. Popular with US and Canadian retirees.
Rentista (Passive Income Visa)For those with passive income (not pension): $2,500/month OR one-time bank deposit of $60,000. 2-year permit, renewable. Must reside 183 days/year.
Inversionista (Investor Visa)Investment of $150,000+ in Costa Rican property, business or government bonds. Permanent residency. いいえ minimum stay requirement.
Digital いいえmad VisaFor remote workers. Income $3,000/month (individual) or $4,000/month (family). 1-year permit, renewable once. いいえ local work allowed.
Temporary Resident (Work)Sponsored by a Costa Rican employer. For skilled professionals. 2-year permit.

ステップバイステップ手順

  1. Hire a Costa Rican immigration lawyer (budget $1,500–3,000)
  2. Gather documents: passport, pension/income proof, police clearance, birth certificate, marriage certificate if applicable
  3. Apostille and notarise all foreign documents; translate to Spanish
  4. Submit to DGME (Dirección General de Migración y Extranjería)
  5. Receive initial conditional residency within 3–12 months
  6. Enrol in the CAJA (public health system) — mandatory for all residents
  7. Obtain your DIMEX card (Documento de Identidad Migratoria para Extranjeros)
  8. After 3 years of temporary residency, apply for permanent residency
💡 プロのヒント： The Pensionado visa requires $1,000/month — even a modest US Social Security benefit often qualifies. The mandatory CAJA enrolment (~$50–150/month) gives access to Costa Rica's excellent public health system.

🏥 コスタリカの医療

公的医療

Costa Rica's CAJA (Caja 費用arricense de Seguro Social) is widely regarded as one of the best public healthcare systems in Latin America. All legal residents must enrol and pay monthly contributions (~$50–150/month based on income). Covers doctor visits, hospitalisation, surgery, maternity and prescription medicines.

民間医療

Private clinics and hospitals operate alongside the public system. CIMA Hospital (San José), Clínica Bíblica, Clínica Católica and Hospital Clínica Bíblica offer English-speaking doctors and US-comparable standards at 40–60% lower costs.

ℹ️ 推奨: Enrol in CAJA immediately — it's mandatory and provides comprehensive coverage for a modest monthly fee. Add a private supplementary plan for faster access to specialists and English-speaking doctors.

🛡️ Supplementary Health Insurance

While the CAJA public system is comprehensive, wait times for non-emergency specialist care and some procedures can be long. Supplementary private insurance provides immediate access to private hospitals and is also useful for the period before CAJA enrolment is active.

🏦 コスタリカでの銀行口座開設

A Costa Rican bank account is needed to pay rent, utilities and CAJA contributions, and to receive local salary or pension transfers. The process has become more regulated but remains manageable.

おすすめ銀行

Banco de Costa Rica (BCR) — State bank. Most expat-friendly, good English service in Escazú/San José. Accepts tourist visas for basic accounts.
Banco Nacional de Costa Rica — Largest state bank. Lowest fees. Extensive branch network throughout the country.
BAC San José (BAC Credomatic) — Best digital banking. Popular with younger expats and digital nomads. Requires DIMEX card.
Scotiabank Costa Rica — Canadian bank, familiar to いいえrth American expats. International wire capabilities.
Prival Bank — Good for higher-net-worth expats and investors. English-speaking staff.

必要書類

  • Valid passport
  • DIMEX card (for residents) — OR tourist entry for basic account at state banks
  • Proof of address (rental contract or utility bill)
  • Proof of income (pension letter, employment contract, bank statements)
  • Costa Rican phone number

ステップバイステップ手順

  1. Visit bank branch in person
  2. For state banks: some accept tourist status for a basic account
  3. Present all documents and complete KYC form
  4. Account typically opened within 1–5 business days
  5. Receive debit card and online banking access
💡 プロのヒント： Banco de Costa Rica in Escazú and Los Yoses (San José) have dedicated expat advisors with strong English skills. State banks (BCR, Banco Nacional) are the most accessible for new arrivals without a DIMEX card.

🏠 コスタリカでの不動産購入

Costa Rica has no restrictions on foreign property ownership — foreigners have the same rights as citizens. The property market is well-established, with a large inventory of expat-friendly properties particularly in the Central Valley, Guanacaste, Manuel Antonio and the Southern Zone.

Options for Foreigners

Titled (Freehold) Property
Full ownership registered at the National Registry. Standard for most urban and suburban properties. Most secure form of ownership.
Concession Land (Maritime Zone)
Land within 200m of the ocean. The first 50m from the high tide line is public and inalienable. The next 150m requires a concession from the municipality. Foreigners can hold concessions but must have 5 years legal residency or go through a Costa Rican company.
Rights of Way / Possession Rights
Untitled land. Very risky for foreigners — avoid unless you have expert legal advice.

購入手順

  1. Hire a Costa Rican real estate lawyer (not optional)
  2. Obtain an RNPN (National Property Registry) title search
  3. Check for liens, easements, back taxes, survey maps
  4. Sign a Purchase Option contract (Opción de Compra) — pay $5,000–20,000 deposit
  5. Complete due diligence: survey, environmental restrictions, zoning
  6. Sign Escritura de Compraventa (Purchase Deed) before a いいえtary Public
  7. Register at the National Registry (Registro Nacional)
  8. Pay transfer taxes and notary fees at closing

Typical 費用s

💡 プロのヒント： Always verify the title at the National Registry (Registro Nacional) yourself or via your lawyer before signing anything. Maritime zone properties near the beach require special legal expertise — do not purchase without understanding the concession terms.

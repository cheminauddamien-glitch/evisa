Turkey eVisa Requirements 2026: Documents, Eligibility & How to Apply

Planning to visit Turkey in 2026? This guide covers every document you need, who is eligible, and the exact steps to apply for a Turkey eVisa.

Overview: Turkey Visa & Entry Requirements 2026

Visa-free for EU citizens and many others; eVisa required for ~100 nationalities. The eVisa system makes Turkey highly accessible — apply online at evisa.gov.tr.

The official fee is USD 50–90 (varies by nationality) with a standard processing time of Instant to 5 minutes. The permitted stay is up to 30 days. Entry is Single or double entry. Currency in use: TRY (Turkish Lira).

Who Is Eligible for a Turkey Visa?

Eligibility: ~100 nationalities eligible for eVisa.

Special note: Visa-free for EU citizens and many others; eVisa required for ~100 nationalities.

Children under 18 must travel with a valid passport of their own. Minors travelling without both parents may need a notarised parental consent letter. Always verify current rules at the official Turkey immigration authority before travel.

How to Apply: Step-by-Step for Turkey in 2026

  1. Check your nationality's eligibility — Visit evisa.gov.tr to confirm your passport is among the ~100 nationalities eligible for a Turkey eVisa and note your specific fee (USD 50 or USD 90).
  2. Prepare your travel documents — Have your valid passport (6+ months validity), a confirmed return or onward ticket, and your accommodation address in Turkey ready before starting the application.
  3. Complete the application form — Enter your personal details, passport information, travel dates, and accommodation at evisa.gov.tr. The form takes approximately 3–5 minutes to complete.
  4. Pay the visa fee online — Pay USD 50 or USD 90 using Visa or MasterCard. Your eVisa will be issued within seconds to minutes after successful payment.
  5. Print and carry your eVisa — Download the eVisa PDF from the confirmation email and print it. Present the printed eVisa alongside your passport at Turkish immigration upon arrival.

Apply via the official portal: evisa.gov.tr. Avoid third-party agents who charge unnecessary service fees on top of the official government fee.

健康・入国要件

  • Medical exam: Required for work permits and residence permits exceeding 1 year. Must be performed at a Turkish public hospital.
  • Health insurance: Mandatory for all residence permit applications. Must be from a Turkish-approved insurer or SGK (Social Security) enrollment for work permit holders.
  • Vaccinations: No mandatory vaccinations for entry.
  • Police clearance: Required for work permits and long-term residence — apostilled criminal record from home country.
  • Biometrics: Fingerprints and photo collected at residence permit appointment at the Provincial Directorate of Migration Management (Il Goc Idaresi).

ビザ延長と超過滞在

  • e-Visa / tourist visa: Cannot be extended. Must exit and re-enter or apply for a residence permit.
  • Residence permit: Apply for renewal online through e-ikamet.goc.gov.tr at least 60 days before expiry. Short-term permits renewable up to a total of 8 years, then eligible for long-term permit.
  • Work permit: Renewable through employer application. After 8 years of legal work: eligible for indefinite work permit.
  • Overstay: Fines and deportation. Overstay exceeding the administrative fine period results in entry bans (1-5 years). Voluntary departure within 10 days may reduce penalties.

拒否と不服申立て

  • Common refusal reasons: Insufficient funds, criminal record, security concerns, incomplete documentation, previous overstay in Turkey.
  • Appeal: e-Visa refusals have no formal appeal. Embassy visa refusals can be reconsidered by submitting additional documents. Residence permit refusals can be appealed to administrative courts within 60 days.
  • Reapplication: Can reapply for e-Visa or embassy visa immediately. Address refusal reasons in the new application.
